### Douglas County Permit Requirements

Douglas County requires a building permit for basement finishing projects. This applies to framing, electrical, plumbing, and HVAC work — any structural or mechanical changes to the space require inspection and approval before the project can be considered complete. This is standard practice and exists to protect homeowners and ensure the work meets building code.

CID Basements handles all permitting and inspections on your behalf. We prepare and submit the required documentation, coordinate with Douglas County Building Division, and schedule the necessary inspections at each phase of the project. You don't need to navigate the permitting process yourself — we take care of it from start to finish.

Hiring a contractor who understands Douglas County's permitting requirements matters. Unpermitted work can create problems when you sell your home and may need to be torn out and redone at your expense. Working with CID means your finished basement is done right, documented, and fully compliant.

Yes. Douglas County requires a building permit for basement finishing, covering framing, electrical, plumbing, and HVAC. Inspections are required at multiple stages.
